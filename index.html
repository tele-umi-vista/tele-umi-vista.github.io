Abstract

Universal Manipulation Interface (UMI) enables scalable real-world robot data collection without hardware-specific teleoperation, yet leveraging UMI data to train large-scale Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models remains fundamentally challenging.

We identify two critical mismatches: visual grounding—wrist-mounted fisheye views with severe radial distortion are out-of-distribution for pretrained VLMs; and physical plausibility—human-collected trajectories frequently violate kinematic limits, incur collisions, or exceed controller bandwidth.

We present UMI-π, bridging this dual gap through three synergistic components: UMI-VQA (8M wrist-fisheye VQA samples), a physical-validation pipeline (reachability, collision, tracking via Mink replay), and two-stage co-training (mixed VQA + discrete action AR, then flow-matching action expert). UMI-π significantly outperforms π0.5, LingBot, and Wall-X on simulation and 20 real-world tasks.

The UMI-π Framework

Perception alignment · Physical validation · Two-stage co-training

Fig. 1 · System Overview

UMI-VQA → Physical Validation → Two-Stage Training → Deployment

Executable Data Validation · Mink Replay · Flow-Matching Action Expert

01

UMI-VQA · Perception Alignment

8M wrist-fisheye vision-language samples aligning the VLM to gripper-centric distorted observations.

  • Five capability subsets: scene captioning, scene-state understanding, object grounding, interaction grounding, spatial reasoning
  • Real UMI demonstration frames + RefSpatial scenes edited via FLUX.2-klein for fisheye domain adaptation
  • Q&A grounded in observable wrist-fisheye evidence: bboxes, affordances, spatial relations
Fig. 3 · UMI-VQA Examples

代表性 Q&A 标注拼图（物体定位 / 场景理解 / 交互区域）

02

Physical Validation

Cross-embodiment trajectory replay in Mink: filter before training, keep score sdc = stc·ssr·ste/10000 ≥ 60.

  • Trajectory continuity — consecutive waypoint distance (position & angle)
  • Self-collision risk — minimum link-link distance during replay
  • Tracking feasibility — end-effector tracking error & implicit workspace limits
Fig. 4 · Validation Flowchart

Raw UMI → Mink Replay (RealMan / AC one / R1 Pro) → Keep / Filter

03

Two-Stage Co-Training

Stage 1: mixed VQA + FAST discrete action tokens (L = Lact + λvqaLvqa). Stage 2: knowledge-isolated flow-matching action expert on validated trajectories.

  • Stage 1 aligns perception and action-token prediction under wrist-fisheye observations
  • Stage 2 trains continuous action chunks without overwriting Stage-1 representations
